Protecting Children from Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a harmful type of bullying that occurs on social media platforms. Typically used to discredit or harm the person recieving the message by sending threats and posting lies. Cyberbullying leaves a digital footprint and can be tracked through social media providing a way to potentially stop it. The best way to prevent cyberbullying is by not responding to the messages and blocking the user entirely.

Definitions

Digital citizen
A person who uses the internet, social media, and technology responsibly, ethically, and safely in the global community.
Digital etiquette
The polite and respectful behavior one should use onlineâ€”how to communicate appropriately in digital spaces.
Online identity
The persona or profile someone creates or presents on the internet, which can include usernames, avatars, and information about oneself.
IP address
A unique numeric label assigned to each device on a network that identifies and allows devices to communicate with each other.
Packet
A small unit of data sent over a network, which travels across the internet in pieces and is reassembled at the destination.
Networks
A system of interconnected devices (computers, servers, routers) that allow data exchange and communication across distances.
Cybersecurity
Practices, technologies, and processes used to protect computers, networks, programs, and data from attack or unauthorized access.
Report
To notify a platform, moderator, or authority about harmful or abusive content or behavior online so action can be taken.
